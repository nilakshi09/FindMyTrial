# FindMyTrial — Clinical Trial Matching in Plain English

## The Problem

There are over **470,000 registered clinical trials** on ClinicalTrials.gov at any given time. Many are actively recruiting patients and offering free treatment, medical monitoring, and in some cases direct compensation of hundreds or thousands of dollars for participation. For someone living with a rare disease or a chronic condition that has stopped responding to standard treatment, a qualifying trial could be genuinely life-changing.

**Almost none of those patients ever find out these trials exist.**

ClinicalTrials.gov was built as a regulatory database for researchers and administrators — not for patients. Its search interface requires you to:

- Already know the clinical terminology for your condition
- Understand what "Phase II" means
- Parse dense eligibility criteria written in medical protocol language
- Manually filter through dozens of results that may be geographically irrelevant or already closed

The average patient gives up within minutes. The information asymmetry is extreme: the data is public, the trials are free to find, and the potential benefit is enormous — but the interface is so hostile to non-experts that the gap between patients and trials stays almost entirely unbridged.

---

## The Solution

**FindMyTrial** lets a user type their situation in plain language — something like:

> *"I have stage 2 lupus and standard treatment stopped working"*

— and does the rest.

### How It Works

1. **Real-time API querying** — Queries the ClinicalTrials.gov API in real time
2. **Smart matching** — Matches against actively recruiting trials
3. **Intelligent filtering** — Filters by location and basic eligibility factors like age and diagnosis stage
4. **Plain English rewriting** — Rewrites each match in clear, accessible language:
   - What researchers are testing
   - What participation involves
   - How long the trial runs
   - Whether travel is required
   - What compensation looks like

### Key Features

- **Natural language search** — No jargon, no manual filtering, no expertise required
- **Save & track trials** — Users can save trials they're interested in
- **Alerts for new matches** — Set notifications for newly posted trials that match your profile
- **Share with your doctor** — Send results directly to your physician for review

---

## Why This Matters

The gap between patients and clinical trials is not a data problem — it's a **design problem**. The information is public. The trials are free to join. The potential benefit is life-changing. What's missing is a bridge between medical protocol language and the real humans who could benefit from it.

FindMyTrial is that bridge.
